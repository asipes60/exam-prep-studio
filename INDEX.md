## QUICK REFERENCE: EXAM FACTS AT A GLANCE

### California Law & Ethics Exam (All 3 Licenses)
- **75 questions** (50 scored, 25 pretest)
- **90 minutes**
- **~70% passing score** (~35/50 correct)
- Administered by: **Pearson VUE**
- Covers: California laws, HIPAA, mandated reporting, confidentiality, ethics codes
- Ethics codes differ by license: NASW (LCSW) | AAMFT/CAMFT (LMFT) | ACA (LPCC)
- Retake: 90-day wait + 12 hours additional L&E coursework

### NCMHCE — National Clinical Mental Health Counseling Exam (LPCC)
- **11 case studies** (~130–150 questions total; 100 scored)
- **255 minutes** (4 hours 15 min)
- Case-based, application-focused — NOT a recall exam
- 5 content domains: Professional Practice/Ethics, Intake/Assessment/Diagnosis, Treatment Planning, Counseling Skills/Interventions, Core Counseling Attributes
- First-attempt pass rate: **~55–60%** — requires thorough preparation
- Administered by: **NBCC / Pearson VUE**

### California LMFT Clinical Exam (LMFT)
- **150 questions** (125 scored, 25 pretest) — updated September 2024
- **4 hours**
- **~67–70% passing score** (varies by form)
- Content: Clinical assessment, treatment planning, crisis management, law/ethics, MFT theories
- Heavy emphasis on: Bowen, Structural, EFT, Gottman, Narrative, SFBT, DSM-5
- Administered by: **BBS / Pearson VUE**

### ASWB Clinical Exam (LCSW)
- **170 questions** (150 scored, 20 pretest)
- **4 hours**
- **Three-option (A/B/C) multiple choice** — updated format
- 4 domains: Human Development/Diversity (24%), Assessment/Diagnosis/Treatment Planning (30%), Psychotherapy/Interventions/Case Management (27%), Professional Values/Ethics (19%)
- First-attempt pass rate: **~76%**
- In-person only at Pearson VUE centers (as of March 31, 2025)
- Key resources: NASW Code of Ethics, DSM-5-TR, ASWB official content outlines

## KEY THEMES ACROSS ALL EXAMS

These themes appear on every exam and should be core study areas regardless of which license a student is pursuing:

1. **Confidentiality and its limits** — when disclosure is required, permitted, or prohibited
2. **Mandated reporting** — child abuse, elder/dependent adult abuse, duty to warn
3. **Tarasoff duty to warn/protect** — California-specific application
4. **HIPAA** — psychotherapy notes, records access, Notice of Privacy Practices
5. **Informed consent** — who, when, what it must include
6. **Dual relationships and boundaries** — ethics code requirements
7. **DSM-5-TR** — major depressive disorder, anxiety disorders, PTSD, bipolar, personality disorders, substance use
8. **Evidence-based treatments** — CBT, DBT, EMDR, MI, trauma-focused approaches
9. **Crisis assessment and intervention** — suicide risk, homicide risk, safety planning
10. **Professional ethics codes** — NASW (LCSW), AAMFT/CAMFT (LMFT), ACA (LPCC)

## AI CURRICULUM GENERATION GUIDE

When using this library to create study content, use the following approach by exam type:

### For California Law & Ethics (all licenses):
→ Reference: `01_California_Law_and_Ethics/CA_Law_Ethics_Complete_Guide.md`
- Focus on the license-specific ethics code differences
- Emphasize scenarios over rote memorization
- Prioritize: mandatory reporting, confidentiality, Tarasoff, HIPAA, minors' rights

### For NCMHCE (LPCC clinical exam):
→ Reference: `02_NCMHCE_LPCC/NCMHCE_Complete_Guide.md`
- Always frame content as clinical case scenarios, not fact-recall
- Include the "less is more" test-taking strategy in any NCMHCE curriculum
- Prioritize: DSM-5 presentations, treatment selection, clinical reasoning

### For California LMFT Clinical Exam:
→ Reference: `03_LMFT_Clinical_Exam/LMFT_Clinical_Exam_Complete_Guide.md`
- Emphasize family systems theories and how they differ
- Include couples therapy models (EFT, Gottman) prominently
- Prioritize: scenario-based theory identification, crisis management, ethical dilemmas

### For ASWB Clinical (LCSW exam):
→ Reference: `04_ASWB_Clinical_LCSW/ASWB_Clinical_Complete_Guide.md`
- Always frame through the Person-in-Environment (PIE) and social work values lens
- NASW Code of Ethics = highest priority content
- Prioritize by domain weight: Assessment/Diagnosis (30%) → Interventions (27%) → Human Development (24%) → Ethics (19%)
