### Key California Laws to Know (Free Reference)

These can be accessed directly from California legislative sites (leginfo.legislature.ca.gov):

- **California Welfare and Institutions Code (WIC)** — particularly:
  - §5150 (involuntary hold criteria)
  - §5585 (minor involuntary holds)
  - §15600+ (elder/dependent adult abuse reporting)
  - §300+ (child abuse and neglect)
- **California Business and Professions Code** — licensing regulations for BBS professions
- **California Penal Code §11164–11174** — Child Abuse and Neglect Reporting Act (CANRA)
- **California Evidence Code §1010–1028** — psychotherapy privilege
- **California Civil Code §56+** — Confidentiality of Medical Information Act (CMIA)
- **California Health & Safety Code §123110** — patient access to records

## STUDY PLANNING RECOMMENDATIONS

### How Long to Study
| Exam | Recommended Study Time |
|---|---|
| California Law & Ethics | 2–4 weeks (focused study) |
| NCMHCE (LPCC) | 2–4 months (case-based; needs immersion) |
| CA LMFT Clinical Exam | 2–4 months (broad clinical content) |
| ASWB Clinical (LCSW) | 2–4 months (broad clinical + DSM + ethics) |

### Study Schedule Framework (per exam)
- **Week 1:** Review content outline; read through complete study guide; identify weak areas
- **Weeks 2–4:** Deep dive into content domains by percentage weight (highest first)
- **Weeks 5–8:** Practice questions and simulations; review rationales carefully
- **Week before exam:** Full-length timed practice exam; review errors; rest
- **Day before:** Light review of key facts only; get good sleep

### Top Universal Tips From Test-Passers
1. Study the content, then the questions — not the other way around
2. Know WHY the right answer is right AND why the wrong answers are wrong
3. Timed practice builds stamina for 4-hour exams
4. Form study groups — teaching others solidifies knowledge
5. Self-care during study period is not optional — your mental bandwidth matters
6. If you fail, analyze which domains were weakest and focus the retake on those
